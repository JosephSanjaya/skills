# Title Naming Patterns

> Generate 5 options in Phase 1. Avoid clickbait and vague nouns.

---

## 1. Attributes of Great Engineering Titles

A strong technical title on Medium must:
1. **Name the specific technology or problem:** Never use vague generalizations.
2. **Signal a concrete lesson or outcome:** Show the value, not just the topic.
3. **Be concise:** Target under 12 words.
4. **Be search-optimized:** Write the title such that someone googling the problem would click it.
5. **Avoid marketing jargon:** No "unlocking the power," "leveraging," or "revolutionary."

---

## 2. Naming Templates

Use these 5 patterns to generate options:

### Pattern 1: [Technology]: [Outcome / Lesson Learned]
*Uses a colon to separate the tech from the practical outcome.*
- **Format:** `[Tech Stack/Project]: [What You Discovered or Built]`
- **Real Examples:**
  - *Medium Android App — Migrating from Apollo Kotlin 3 to 4: Lessons Learned*
  - *Scaling ArchUnit with Nebula ArchRules*
  - *Thinking Fast & Slow for a Personalized Notification System*

### Pattern 2: How We [Did X]: [Surprising Method or Angle]
*Active voice. Puts the engineering team in the driver's seat.*
- **Format:** `How We [Verb + Object]: [Specific Angle]`
- **Real Examples:**
  - *Making AI Write Android Code Our Way: A Practical Guide to Agent Skills*
  - *How Pandao Uses the In-App Updates Flexible Flow to Speed up the App Update Process on Android*

### Pattern 3: Scaling [Concept] [at/with] [Scale/Context]
*For architectural, infrastructure, or data platform systems.*
- **Format:** `Scaling [Core Concept]: [What You Did With It]`
- **Real Examples:**
  - *Scaling Global Storytelling: Modernizing Localization Analytics at Netflix*
  - *Scaling ArchUnit with Nebula ArchRules*

### Pattern 4: The Counterintuitive Claim
*Lead with a short, polarizing assertion that challenges conventions.*
- **Format:** `[Short assertion that challenges expectations]`
- **Examples:**
  - *We Split Our Notification System in Two. It Made Everything Faster.*
  - *Our Android Team Has 4 Engineers and 13 AI Agents.*

### Pattern 5: [Before State] → [After State]
*Ideal for migrations, performance optimizations, and debugging stories.*
- **Format:** `From [Before State] to [After State]: [How/Why]`
- **Examples:**
  - *From CacheMissException to Cache-First: Our Apollo Kotlin 4 Migration*
  - *From 13 Skills to One AGENTS.md: How We Taught AI Our Android Codebase*

---

## 3. Anti-Patterns (Titles to avoid)

| Bad Title | Why it fails |
| :--- | :--- |
| *“A Deep Dive into Apollo Kotlin”* | "Deep dive" is a cliché; names no lesson. |
| *“Exploring the World of Android”* | Vague; lacks a concrete problem. |
| *“How We Improved App Performance”* | No specific technology, metrics, or details. |
| *“The Complete Guide to X”* | Overpromises; reads like generic SEO spam. |
| *“Unlocking the Power of Git”* | Marketing terminology; lacks engineering tone. |
